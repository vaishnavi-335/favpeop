My Favourite People

Dr. Vikas Divyakirti

Vikas Divyakirti is a renowned Indian educator, author, and entrepreneur best known as the founder and managing director of Drishti IAS,one of India’s leading UPSC coaching institutes based in Delhi. Born on December 26, 1973, in Bhiwani, Haryana, Divyakirti’s academic journey began in a Hindi-medium school and later led him to Delhi University, where he pursued degrees in commerce, history, law, and ultimately earned a Ph.D. in Hindi Literature. He also studied subjects like sociology, philosophy, and management, reflecting his broad intellectual interests Divyakirti started his professional career as an assistant professor at Delhi University. In 1996, he cleared the UPSC Civil Services Examination on his first attempt and joined the Central Secretariat Service, working in the Ministry of Home Affairs. However, his passion for teaching prompted him to resign after a year and focus on education full-time. In 1999, he established Drishti IAS, which has since become a major institution for civil services preparation, known for its rigorous academic environment and a strong presence on social media and YouTube, where his educational videos have garnered millions of followers Beyond teaching, Divyakirti is recognized for promoting a culture of open discourse on social and political issues, encouraging critical thinking over rote consensus. He has also appeared as himself in the film 12th Fail, contributing to its script and highlighting the struggles of aspirants from modest backgrounds

Virat Kohali

Virat Kohli is one of the most accomplished and influential cricketers of his generation, celebrated for his extraordinary batting, leadership, and consistency across all formats. Born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, Kohli rose to prominence after captaining India’s Under-19 team to victory in the 2008 World Cup. He soon became a mainstay in the Indian national side, earning a reputation for his aggressive style and relentless pursuit of excellence. Kohli’s career is marked by a series of remarkable records. He has scored over 27,500 runs in international cricket, making him the third-highest run-scorer in history, only behind Sachin Tendulkar and Kumar Sangakkara. He holds the record for the most ODI centuries (50), surpassing even Tendulkar, and is the fastest to reach several batting milestones, including 8,000, 10,000, 12,000, and 13,000 ODI runs. In T20 internationals, he has amassed over 4,000 runs with the highest career average in the format (52.73).As captain, Kohli led India to 40 Test wins out of 68 matches, the most by any Indian captain. He has been instrumental in India’s major tournament victories, including the 2011 World Cup, 2013 and 2025 Champions Trophies, and the 2024 T20 World Cup. Kohli is also a stalwart in the Indian Premier League, holding the record for most runs in IPL history and winning the title with Royal Challengers Bangalore after 18 seasons. His achievements have been recognized with numerous awards, including the Arjuna Award, Padma Shri, and the Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna, as well as multiple ICC Cricketer of the Year honors. Off the field, Kohli is known for his fitness revolution in Indian cricket and his philanthropic and business ventures, further cementing his legacy as a modern sporting icon.

Katrina Kaif

Katrina Kaif is a popular actress in the Indian film industry. She was born on July 16, 1983, in Hong Kong. Her mother is British, and her father is from India. Katrina grew up in different countries like China, Japan, France, and England before moving to India for her career.She started her career as a model and later became an actress. Her first Bollywood movie was “Boom” in 2003, but it didn’t do well. She got famous after her movie “Maine Pyaar Kyun Kiya” with Salman Khan. After that, she acted in many hit movies like “Namastey London,” “Singh is Kinng,” “Zindagi Na Milegi Dobara,” “Ek Tha Tiger,” “Jab Tak Hai Jaan,” and “Tiger Zinda Hai.”Katrina is known for her beautiful looks, graceful dancing, and hard work. Even though Hindi was not her first language, she practiced a lot to speak well in her movies. People love her for her dedication and improvement over time.She also started her own beauty brand called Kay Beauty, which became popular in India. Katrina is also involved in charity work and supports many causes.In 2021, Katrina married actor Vicky Kaushal, and fans were very happy for them.Katrina Kaif is one of the most successful actresses in Bollywood today. Her journey from a foreign model to a top Indian actress is very inspiring. She proved that with hard work, focus, and a positive attitude, anything is possible.

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was a great scientist and the 11th President of India. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. His full name was Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam. His family was not rich, but he was very smart and worked hard from a young age. Kalam studied aeronautical engineering. He loved science and wanted to help India grow in technology. He joined ISRO (Indian Space Research Organisation) and worked on many space and missile projects. That’s why people called him the "Missile Man of India."He helped in making Agni and Prithvi missiles. He also worked on India’s nuclear tests in 1998 at Pokhran. These made India stronger in defense. His work made all Indians proud. In 2002, he became the President of India. He was a kind and simple person. He loved meeting students and always told them to dream big and work hard. People called him the “People’s President” because he was loved by everyone.Even after his time as President, he kept teaching and giving speeches to students. He believed that education and youth are the future of India. He wrote many books, like "Wings of Fire", "Ignited Minds", and "India 2020". These books inspire young people to follow their dreams. Dr. Abdul Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while giving a speech to students in Shillong. He died doing what he loved—teaching. Dr. Kalam is remembered as a great scientist, a true patriot, and a kind human being. His life teaches us to be humble, positive, and to never give up.

Shrushti Jayant Deshmukh

Shrushti Jayant Deshmukh is an Indian IAS officer. She became famous after passing the UPSC exam in 2018. She got All India Rank 5 and was the topper among girls that year.Shrushti was born on 28 March 1995 in Madhya Pradesh. Her father is an engineer, and her mother is a teacher. From a young age, Shrushti was a bright student. She studied in a private school and later completed chemical engineering from Rajiv Gandhi Proudyogiki Vishwavidyalaya (RGPV) in Bhopal.Even while studying engineering, she had a strong interest in civil services. She prepared for UPSC along with her college. She did not take any coaching; instead, she studied with the help of books, online videos, and self-study. Her hard work, focus, and discipline helped her clear the exam in her first attempt, which is a big achievement.Shrushti chose sociology as her optional subject. She believes that time management and consistency are very important for success. In many interviews, she has inspired lakhs of students by sharing her journey. After becoming an IAS officer, she got training at LBSNAA (Lal Bahadur Shastri National Academy of Administration). She later got posted in Madhya Pradesh cadre. In 2022, she married IAS officer Nagarjun B Gowda.Shrushti is not only intelligent but also humble and hardworking. Many students look up to her as a role model. Her success story shows that with dedication and self-belief, you can achieve anything.She continues to work for the people and serve the country with pride. Her life is a strong example for girls and boys who dream of becoming IAS officers.

Sachin Tendulkar

Sachin Tendulkar is one of the greatest cricketers in the world. He is also known as the "Master Blaster" and the "God of Cricket." He was born on April 24, 1973, in Mumbai, India.Sachin started playing cricket at a very young age. His coach Ramakant Achrekar saw his talent and trained him well. Sachin worked very hard and played with full focus. He played his first international match for India when he was only 16 years old.He played cricket for 24 years and made many world records. He is the only player to score 100 international centuries and the first to make 200 runs in a one-day match. He also scored over 34,000 runs in international cricket.Sachin played in six World Cups, and in 2011, he was part of the Indian team that won the World Cup. That moment was very special for him and the country.He was very humble and respectful, both on and off the field. People loved him not only for his cricket but also for his good behavior and simple life. Young cricketers see him as an inspiration. Sachin retired from cricket in 2013. After his last match, the whole stadium and fans across the world gave him a big farewell. In 2014, he received the Bharat Ratna, India’s highest civilian award. He was the youngest person to get it.Now, Sachin works in sports development and also supports many charity causes. He still inspires millions of people, especially young cricketers.Sachin Tendulkar’s life teaches us to follow our passion, stay humble, and never stop trying. He is truly a legend of Indian cricket.
